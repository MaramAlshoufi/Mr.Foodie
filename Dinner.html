Pick Any Meal You Want And Order It Now From Mr.Foodie

The recipes below include several non-vegetarian options, which are not traditional.
But rest assured, we’ve included plenty of vegetarian dishes as well,
so there’s something for everyone to enjoy, including cheesy appetizers and hearty mains.

Chicken Yakhni Pulao

The essence of this pulao is the yakhni, which means meat stock or broth: The delicate flavor of the chicken stock is absorbed by the rice. Do use chicken thighs on the bone when preparing the stock rather than boneless thighs or breasts to ensure the stock has lots of flavor.

Cheesy Samosa Puffs

Snacks are a crucial component of every Diwali party, and these samosa puffs increase the fun factor, with light and crispy pastry encasing a soft, cheesy interior. The cheddar cheese melts into the spiced potato mash once baked. Serve with your choice of hot sauce or green chutney for an added punch, and this is sure to become one of your favorite Diwali recipes.

Ghurma Aloo

This quick stew of pan-fried potatoes, tomatoes, and onions flavored with whole cumin seeds and ground turmeric comes from Raghavan Iyer's 660 Curries.

Paneer Butter Masala

This deeply satisfying recipe for butter paneer comes from Meera Sodha's Fresh India. Here, cubes of creamy paneer simmer in a tomato-based sauce that's been enlivened with ginger, garlic, cinnamon, cloves, fenugreek, and more.

Stir Fried Prawns

The prawns in Nellore make it to the kitchen literally minutes after they are caught, and really do taste like the sea. But this dish is still delicious if you make it elsewhere. The trick to this recipe is to be patient and allow the onions to brown to a deep, caramelized sweetness.

Chickpea-Potato Chaat

For an easy, low-cook dish that everyone adores, toss canned chickpeas with potatoes, cilantro-mint chutney, tamarind chutney, and yogurt, then top with chiles, onions, tomatoes, and chaat masala.

Chile & Yogurt Marinated Grilled Checken

Eating meat isn't traditional for Diwali, but if you do partake, may we make the case for this chicken dish from London restaurant, Gunpowder? Just marinate chicken thighs in a powerfully aromatic yogurt sauce and then let them get nice and charred on the grill.

Zucchini Lentil Fritters With Lemony Yogurt

Sohla El-Waylly's crispy zucchini fritters take inspiration from Bengali piyaju. The oniony batter is made from soaked, puréed red lentils and is spiked with turmeric and chile powder.

Down South Goat Biryani

Executive Editor Sonia Chopra calls biryani “one of the ultimate celebration dishes in India." Asha Gomez's version, from her first cookbook, My Two Souths, layers goat and aromatics with fluffy basmati rice.

Paneer with Burst Cherry Tomato Sauce

This take on matar paneer swaps out the typical shelled peas for sugar snap peas, along with a handful of cherry tomatoes and thick planks of seared paneer.

Yogurt Braised Chicken Legs With Garlic And Ginger

Chicken legs are seared until golden-brown and then slowly braised in yogurt spiked with garlic, ginger, and lots of spices until falling-off-the-bone tender. Serve with naan or another flatbread for dipping into the creamy yogurt sauce.

Sonoran Style Potato, Cheese And Tomato Soup

This is Epi contributor Christian Reynoso's version of an undersung regional Mexican dish that’s deeply satisfying yet remarkably light. In Spanish it’s called caldo de queso, and it features chunks of potato simmered in tomato-tinted broth until soft, with fresh cheese such as queso fresco added to each bowl.

Miso-Squash Ramen

Noodle soup recipes don't have to start with chicken. This ramen, for example, gets its rich flavor from squash that's rubbed in miso before roasting. We can't get enough of this fall soup from Epi contributor Hetty McKinnon.

Spicy Coconut Pumpkin Soup

This coconutty soup from Epi contributor Ramin Ganeshram is ideal for fall, when squash and pumpkin are readily available. Serve it with crusty bread or a hearty green salad for the perfect autumn lunch.

Cioppino With Gremolata Toast

Cioppino is the perfect excuse to buy whatever seafood looks best at the market. It works well with clams, shrimp, mussels, and squid, plus flaky white fish such as bass, halibut, hake, or cod, but you can improvise a bit based on what's available.
